Here's a little more about my trip

After many years of working 2 jobs year round, I started to feel the need for a change, I was burned out. I started to do some research and found a way to travel for long periods of time without spending much money.

That's when I came across volunteering platforms like Workaway and WWOOF. It's a great way to exchange up to 5 hours a day of work for food and shelter. Everything is safe and verified and from personal experience it was great. I met great people, I helped families in many different ways and I always had time to travel around after a days work.

I volunteered mainly in farms, I pulled weeds, dug a pond, planted trees, built benches and tables, I even worked cleaning rooms on a hotel in Malaysia. This helped me grow as a person, helped me realize I can do many things not just take order at a restaurant. The best part was watching a family moving into their dream home, cooking the veggies I planted, helping a small hotel owner, eating eggplant parmeggiana with an italian chef, hiking above the clouds in taiwan, etc

Of course there's a lot of planning involved, and even then things might not always workout the way you want it. But that's the beauty of having no timelines or responsabilities when you just take everythingyou own, pack it up and hit the road. Sometimes In had to sleep in the airport, got a fever after getting the yellow fever vaccine to travel to south america, I wasted a few plane tickets but after every single struggle, the pleasure of meeting new places, cultures, pleople would always be worth the troubles.

I you're reading and would like my two cents about travelling and volunteering here it is:

Always research visas for all the countries you travel to and/or if you need a visa to transit to the country.

Never wait until the last minute to book a Workaway.

Get the yellow fever vaccine when going to south america because they might not ask for it when you get there but they will ask for it when you try to leave.

There are other platforms like Couchsurfing that you can use to get free accomodation but you might have to sleep in a sleeping bag or a couch.

The cheapest option is not always the best option.

Research transportation within the country you are travelling to.

Look up online or ask in forums what are the sketchy areas in the place you are travelling to.
